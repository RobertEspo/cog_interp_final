Interpreting Experience Mediates Recruitment of Early Intonational Cues to Sentence Modality: An Eye Tracking Study

Robert Esposito

Rutgers University

2025-12-07

Cues to sentence modality

Two pre-final cues

  1. first peak scaling
  2. presence or absence of medial pitch accent

Face (2005, 2007)

Prediction

Prediction in the L1

Humans constantly predict non-linguistic (Bar, 2007; Lee & Mumford, 2003) and linguistic information (Altmann & Kamide, 1999; Van Petten & Luka, 2012)

Extant research suggests that intonational information is processed in real-time (Crocker, 2010; Esteve-Gibert et al., 2020; Grodner et al., 2010; Kamide et al., 2003; Levy, 2008)

Prediction in the L2

L1 German L2 English [CEFR B2 or above; Europe (2001)] learners predict referent based on contrastive pitch accent in L1, but not in L2 for the same sound-to-meaning prediction (Foltz, 2021)

In contrast, Klassen (2015) found that L1 Spanish L2 English learners predict referent based on contrastive pitch accent in L2

Prediction in the L2

The cues matched in the L1 and L2 in both studies… so why the difference?

  • The Spanish-English bilinguals had more time to predict in the task (Foltz, 2021)?
  • Difference in proficiency?
  • Difference in language exposure?

Prediction in Interpreters

  • Interpreters are seen as expert predictors (Dong & Li, 2020; Setton et al., 2005)
  • We can use interpreters to control for proficiency so that we can look at language exposure
  • Interpreters are expected to take advantage of all aspects of the signal, including prosody (Gile, 1992; Kohn & Kalina, 1996; Riccardi, 1997), but there is a lack of experimental research

Prosody and Prediction in Interpreters

  • Seeber (2001) tasked German-English interpreters with monotonous vs lively source texts and examined rates of prediction and accuracy of verb, but found no difference
  • Unfortunately, the connection between intonation and verb prediction was not motivated and sample size was small
  • L1 English L2 Spanish interpreters make use of first syllable’s prosodic pattern to predict suffix (Lozano-Argüelles et al., 2020, 2023; Lozano-Argüelles & Sagarra, 2021)

Language Exposure, Prediction, & Interpreters

  • The more often one (accurately) predicts, the more frequently they will make the same predictions based on the same cues (Altmann & Kamide, 1999; Bar, 2007; Grüter & Kaan, 2021; Lee & Mumford, 2003; Roettger & Franke, 2019; Van Petten & Luka, 2012)

Research questions

  1. Do L1 Spanish monolinguals recruit early intonational cues (i.e., first F0 peak and presence/absence of medial pitch accent) to predict sentence modality?

  2. Do L1 English L2 Spanish late learners recruit early intonational cues (i.e., first F0 speak and presence/absence of medial pitch accent) to predict sentence modality?

  3. Is the recruitment of the identified early intonational cues in L1 English L2 Spanish late learner dependent on experience interpreting questions from Spanish to English?

Methodology

Participants

  • 30 L1 Madrid Spanish speakers
  • 60 L1 UK English L2 Spanish professional interpreters
    • 30 working in Madrid
    • 30 working in Gibraltr

Materials

  • LexTALE-ESP (Izura et al., 2014)
  • language background questionnaire
  • eye-tracking task
